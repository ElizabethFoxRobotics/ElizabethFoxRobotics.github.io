Project 6 / Deep Learning

Untrained Network

Running the code as given gave 27% accuracy. Adding randomly mirrored images increased the accuracy to 32%, and zero-centering the images increased it further to 40%. Adding in dropout regularization actually decreased the accuracy slightly, to 39%. Adding in another convolution/max pool step in the middle, with some tuning, increased the accuracy to 42%. When batch normalization was added, normalizing the first two of three convolution layers, the process time increased from 200 seconds to 230 seconds, and the accuracy increased to 45%. Increasing the learning rate at this point to 0.001 increased the time to 312 seconds and increased the accuracy to 51%. Increasing the number of epoches to 100 increased the time to 493 seconds and increased the accuracy to 54%. Figure 1 shows the filter visualizations for the ten filters learned through the process, and Figure 2 shows the plots of the accuracies of the test and validation sets over the epochs.

Trained Network

The first training round, setting up the system as instructed, with 10 epoches as provided in the starter code, took 1555 seconds and gave 88.3% accuracy. Figure 3 shows the filter visualizations for the sixty-four filters learned through the process, and Figure 4 shows the plots of the accuracies of the test and validation sets over the epochs. Training with only 5 epochs took 1102 seconds and gave 85.9% accuracy. Training each epoch of the pre-trained network took longer, and the overall process took longer; however, the accuracy was much higher than from the from-scratch network, and it likely would have taken at least as much computational time to get comparable accuracies from scratch.
